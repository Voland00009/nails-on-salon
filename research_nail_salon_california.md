# Executive Summary

This research provides a strategic foundation for building the Nails On Salon website — a first online presence for Tetiana, an independent nail technician operating from a private suite inside Phenix Salon Suites in National City, California.

## Key Strategic Imperatives

1. **Trust-first architecture.** For a solo nail tech without a Google Business Profile or established web presence, every element of the site must build credibility: California license display, professional photography of Tetiana and her studio, portfolio of work, and transparent service information. The private suite model is an advantage when positioned as "exclusive, personalized" rather than "small, hidden."

2. **Instagram DM as primary booking channel requires friction reduction.** With no phone, email, or booking widget, the site must minimize the steps between "I want to book" and "I'm in a DM conversation." The `ig.me/m/nailsonsalon` deep link format (verified working as of February 2026) opens Instagram DM directly. The site needs a clear "How to Book" flow with specific instructions on what to write in the DM.

3. **California ADA compliance is non-negotiable.** In 2025, 5,000+ digital accessibility lawsuits were filed nationally, with a 37% increase year-over-year. California's Unruh Civil Rights Act allows $4,000+ per violation per visit. 67% of ADA website lawsuits targeted businesses with under $25M revenue. WCAG 2.1 AA compliance is a legal requirement, not a nice-to-have. The brand color #FFC2D1 on white fails contrast requirements — text on brand color must always be dark (#1C1018).

4. **Mobile-first is mandatory.** Beauty service websites see 70-85% mobile traffic. The site must be designed for mobile first, with fast load times (LCP ≤ 2.0s), touch-friendly targets (≥ 48px), and a booking CTA visible at all scroll positions.

5. **Premium feminine aesthetic without cliché.** The 2025-2026 trend is "feminine design 2.0" — warm editorial luxury that blends soft elegance with strategic conversion design. Avoid script fonts, excessive pink, sparkle effects, and generic salon templates. Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans is a strong typographic pairing for this positioning.

6. **Local SEO from day one.** Google Business Profile creation, JSON-LD structured data (NailSalon schema), NAP consistency, and local keyword optimization are essential to being found by clients searching "nail salon National City" or "nail salon near me."

7. **Four-page multi-page site is the right architecture.** Home, Services, Gallery, Book — each page serves a distinct intent. A single-page site would sacrifice SEO and make the services section unwieldy. The four-page structure balances depth with simplicity.

## Nails On Salon Positioning

Tetiana's positioning in the market:
- **Price segment:** Mid-to-premium ($60-$130 per service), consistent with independent specialist positioning in San Diego County
- **Differentiators:** Hard Gel Overlay (signature), Smart Touch Dry Pedicure (waterless technique), private suite environment, personalized one-on-one attention

# UX Patterns

## Homepage Structure

### Recommended Section Order

Based on analysis of high-converting beauty service websites, the optimal homepage structure for Nails On Salon:

1. **Hero** — Strong editorial photo of Tetiana (or her work), headline (5-8 words), subheadline with location/specialty, primary CTA ("Book via Instagram")
2. **Social proof strip** — Google rating (once GBP is created), Instagram follower count, years of experience. Even pre-GBP: "Licensed California Nail Technician" + "500+ happy clients" (if accurate)
3. **Services overview** — 3-4 category cards (Manicure, Pedicure, Extensions, Add-ons) with signature service highlighted, "starting from" prices, visual for each
4. **Portfolio gallery strip** — 6-9 best works in a horizontal scroll (mobile) / grid (desktop), link to full gallery
5. **About / Meet Tetiana** — Professional portrait, 2-3 sentences about her approach, specializations, experience
6. **Testimonials** — 2-3 client reviews with names and specifics
7. **How to Book (Instagram CTA)** — Full-width brand-color section, step-by-step booking instructions, primary CTA button
8. **Location + Map** — Address, Google Maps embed, parking info, Phenix Salon Suites mention
9. **FAQ** — 3-5 most common questions in accordion format
10. **Footer** — Navigation, license number, Instagram link, address

### What Strong Operators Do

- Keep hero section focused: one image, one headline, one CTA. No sliders, no auto-carousels
- Social proof strip appears immediately after hero — before the visitor needs to scroll much
- Services section shows enough to create interest, not the full price list (that's on /services)
- Portfolio appears early — nail services are visual, seeing the work is the #1 trust builder
- "How to Book" section appears as a distinct, visually differentiated band (brand color background)
- FAQ addresses booking friction questions: "How do I book?", "How far in advance?", "What's your cancellation policy?"

### Traffic Reality

- Beauty service websites see **70-85% mobile traffic** — one of the most mobile-heavy industries
- Mobile visitors are often in "decision mode" — searching for a nail salon while on-the-go

### Mobile UX Requirements

| Element | Specification | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Touch targets | ≥ 48×48px (Material Design) | Fat finger prevention |
| CTA button height | ≥ 56px for primary CTA | Thumb-zone friendly |
| Body text | ≥ 16px | Prevents iOS zoom on focus |
| Spacing between links | ≥ 8px | Prevents mis-taps |
| Navigation | Hamburger menu + sticky CTA | CTA always visible |
| Gallery | Swipeable grid / horizontal scroll | Touch-native interaction |
| Phone number | Clickable `tel:` link | One-tap calling |
| Address | Clickable → Google Maps | One-tap navigation |
| Instagram CTA | `ig.me/m/` deep link | Opens app directly |

### Navigation Pattern

**Recommended:** Sticky top header with logo + hamburger + CTA button ("Book" / Instagram icon).

Bottom navigation bars are emerging in beauty apps but are unnecessary for a 4-page site. A sticky header with a persistent CTA achieves the same goal with less complexity.

The CTA button should be visually distinct — use the brand color with dark text, or a contrasted accent color.

## Booking Flow: Instagram DM

### The Challenge

Instagram DM as the sole booking method creates friction:
1. Visitor must leave the website
2. Visitor must open Instagram (or install it)
3. Visitor must figure out what to write
4. Visitor doesn't know when to expect a response

### Friction Reduction Strategy

**Deep link format (confirmed working Feb 2026):**
```
https://ig.me/m/nailsonsalon
```

This opens Instagram DM directly with Nails On Salon's chat. On mobile with Instagram app installed, it opens the app. On desktop, opens Instagram web.

**CTA button design:**
- Text: "Book via Instagram" or "Message to Book"
- Include Instagram icon for instant recognition
- Size: ≥ 56px height on mobile
- Visually distinct as primary action

**"How to Book" section — must include:**
1. Three numbered steps with icons:
   - Step 1: "Tap the button below to message me on Instagram"
   - Step 2: "Tell me: what service + your preferred date & time"
   - Step 3: "I'll confirm your spot within [X] hours"
2. Response time promise: "I typically respond within 1-2 hours during business hours"
3. Business hours for responses
4. Pre-fill suggestion: "Just write: 'Hi! I'd like to book [service] on [date]'"
5. Backup contact: email for people without Instagram (accessibility consideration)

**Why it matters:** Without this guidance, the visitor hits a "blank page" in DM and may not follow through. Specific instructions reduce abandonment.

*Source: [ig.me Link Guide](https://creatorflow.so/blog/ig-me-link-guide/), [BotPenguin](https://botpenguin.com/blogs/instagram-direct-message-link)*

### CTA Placement Strategy

CTAs should appear at every "proof point" on the page:

| Location | CTA Type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hero section | Primary button | Above the fold, immediate |
| After portfolio | Text link or button | "Love what you see? Book now" |
| After testimonials | Button | Social proof → action |
| How to Book section | Primary button (largest) | Dedicated section |
| Sticky header | Small button | Always visible |
| Footer | Link | Last chance |

Rule: **4-6 CTAs total** per page (including sticky). Not all identical — vary the wording.

## FAQ & Policies Patterns

### Essential FAQ Questions for Nails On Salon

1. **How do I book an appointment?** (Critical — addresses the DM booking flow)
2. **What's your cancellation policy?** (Builds trust through transparency)
3. **How long does [service] take?** (Sets expectations)
4. **What products do you use?** (Quality reassurance)
5. **Is parking available?** (Practical — Plaza Bonita has ample parking)
6. **Do you accept walk-ins?** (Answer: by appointment only)

### Policy Presentation

**Tone:** Firm but warm. Explain the "why" behind each policy.

Example: "I ask for at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations. As a solo artist, a missed appointment means I can't serve another client in that time slot — and I want to make sure everyone gets the care they deserve."

Format: Accordion/collapsible — visitors can scan headings and expand only what interests them. Never a wall of text.

# Visual & Branding Trends

## 2025-2026 Direction: "Warm Editorial Luxury"

The beauty web design landscape has shifted from sterile minimalism to what the industry calls **"feminine design 2.0"** — soft elegance infused with strategic, editorial storytelling.

**Key characteristics:**

- Template-identical layouts (hero → features → testimonials → CTA)

- Excessive geometric patterns

## Typography: Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans

### Cormorant Garamond

A high-contrast display serif with thin strokes and elegant serifs. Free on Google Fonts. Works beautifully in large sizes (40px+) for a "premium editorial" feel. Italic variant is especially elegant for accent phrases.

**Constraints:**
- Never use below 24px — thin strokes lose legibility at small sizes
- Preferred weights for headings: Medium (500) or SemiBold (600) — Regular (400) is too thin on screen
- Letter-spacing: +0.02em to +0.05em for uppercase use
- Line-height: 1.1 for large headings, 1.2 for subheadings

### DM Sans

A geometric grotesque with soft terminals. Clean, modern, highly readable. Good body font that doesn't compete with the display serif.

**Usage:**
- Body text: 16-18px, Regular (400), line-height 1.6
- UI elements (buttons, navigation): 14-16px, Medium (500)
- Captions/meta: 14px, Regular

### Pairing Hierarchy

| Element | Font | Size (desktop) | Weight | Line-height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero heading (h1) | Cormorant Garamond | 56-72px | 500 | 1.1 |
| Section heading (h2) | Cormorant Garamond | 36-48px | 500 | 1.15 |
| Subsection (h3) | Cormorant Garamond | 24-30px | 600 | 1.2 |
| Body text | DM Sans | 16-18px | 400 | 1.6 |
| Small / meta | DM Sans | 14px | 400 | 1.5 |
| Buttons / nav | DM Sans | 14-16px | 500 | 1 |
| Accent phrase | Cormorant Garamond Italic | 20-24px | 400 | 1.3 |

**Rule:** Never mix both fonts in the same sentence. Cormorant for display, DM Sans for everything interactive and small.

## Color System

### Building from #FFC2D1

The brand color #FFC2D1 is a warm pastel pink (HSL: 346°, 100%, 88%). It's **light** — luminance ~0.62. This means:
- **White text on #FFC2D1 = contrast ratio ~1.5:1 — FAILS WCAG AA** (requires 4.5:1)
- **Dark text (#1C1018) on #FFC2D1 = contrast ratio ~11.7:1 — PASSES WCAG AAA**
- The brand color works as an accent/background, never as a text color on white

4. **No pure black (#000) or pure white (#FFF)** — warm neutrals throughout for cohesion
5. **Gold for "premium touches"** — decorative lines, dividers, subtle accents. Not for text blocks

## Motion & Animation

### When to Animate

- **Page load:** Soft fade-in of hero elements (600-800ms)
- **Scroll reveals:** Sections fade in as they enter viewport
- **Hover effects:** Gallery items scale slightly (1.03), CTA buttons shift color
- **Lightbox:** Smooth open/close transitions for gallery

### When NOT to Animate

- Prices, contact info, navigation — must be instantly available
- Parallax scrolling — causes motion sickness, performance heavy
- Bouncing/pulsing elements — unprofessional
- Animated backgrounds — performance killer
- Auto-play video — frustrating on mobile, wastes data

### Technical Rules

- Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
- Use only `transform` + `opacity` for animations (avoid layout-triggering properties)
- Maximum animation duration: 800ms
- All animations must be non-blocking for Core Web Vitals

# Conversion Strategy

## Trust-Building Hierarchy

Ranked by impact on booking intent for a solo nail technician:

### Tier 1 — Critical (Must-have)

1. **Portfolio of work (high-quality photos)** — Visual proof is the #1 decision factor for nail services. First 3 images determine perception. Show variety of styles.

2. **Real photo of Tetiana** — Clients want to see who will be touching their hands. Professional portrait = human connection + credibility. Solo techs with visible face/name convert better than anonymous brands.

4. **Google/Yelp reviews** — Once GBP is created, displaying star rating + review count is the strongest external trust signal. ~87% of consumers read reviews for local businesses. Until then, use Instagram social proof or client testimonials.

### Tier 2 — High Impact (Should-have)

5. **Studio environment photos** — Show the clean, well-decorated private suite. Addresses sanitation concerns and "is this place legitimate?" anxiety.

6. **Sanitation messaging** — Brief, not clinical: "Single-use files. Sterilized tools. Your safety, always." 3-4 bullet points or icons. Post-COVID, this matters.

7. **Testimonials with specifics** — "Tetiana is the best nail tech I've been to in 5 years" is stronger than "Great service!" Include first name, initial, and specific details.

8. **Response time promise** — "I respond to DMs within 2 hours during business hours" — reduces the anxiety of DM-based booking.

### Tier 3 — Nice-to-have (Optional)

9. **Instagram follower count** — Social proof through audience size
10. **Professional brands used** — "I use [brand name] hard gel systems" — familiar brand names increase confidence
11. **Years of experience** — In bio/about section
12. **Transparent cancellation policy** — Shows professionalism and fairness

```

    ↓
```

### First-Visit Anxiety

New clients may worry about:
- "What will the place look like?" → Studio photos
- "Will she understand what I want?" → Portfolio diversity + "Bring your inspiration" messaging
- "What if I don't like it?" → Satisfaction-oriented language (not guarantee)
- "Is it safe/clean?" → Sanitation bullet points
- "How do I get there?" → Clear address, parking info, map
- "What if she doesn't respond?" → Response time promise

- Clients who see prices and still book are more committed (fewer no-shows)
- Hiding prices signals either "too expensive to show" or "not confident in value"
- Competitors in the area mostly hide prices — showing them is a differentiator

**How:**
- Services page: full menu with exact prices
- Homepage: "Starting from $60" on category cards
- Add-ons: show range ($10-20)
- Include duration alongside price — helps clients plan

# Technical Recommendations

## ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA — California Compliance

### Legal Context

- California's **Unruh Civil Rights Act** allows **$4,000+ in statutory damages per violation per visit**, without requiring proof of actual harm
- In 2025, digital accessibility lawsuits increased **37%** year-over-year, with **5,000+ cases filed** nationally
- **67% of lawsuits targeted businesses with less than $25M annual revenue** — small businesses are primary targets
- California (alongside New York) is the most active state for ADA web lawsuits
- Courts use **WCAG 2.1 Level AA as the de-facto standard**, even though formal Title III rules for private businesses are still pending
- **Overlay widgets don't protect:** 22.6% of sued websites had accessibility overlays installed

*Sources: [UsableNet ADA Trends 2026](https://blog.usablenet.com/ada-web-lawsuit-trends-2026), [WCAG Safe Statistics](https://wcagsafe.com/blog/ada-lawsuit-statistics), [Accessible.org 2026 Predictions](https://accessible.org/2026-ada-website-compliance-lawsuits-ai/)*

### Accessibility Checklist for Nails On Salon

**P1 — Critical (must pass before launch):**
- Color contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for all text (verify brand pink #FFC2D1 — dark text only)
- Alt text on all `<img>` elements (descriptive for portfolio, empty for decorative)
- `<html lang="en">`
- Heading hierarchy: h1 → h2 → h3, no skipped levels, one h1 per page
- Keyboard navigation: all interactive elements reachable via Tab, activatable via Enter/Space
- Visible focus indicators (`:focus-visible` styles — Tailwind removes default outline)
- Semantic HTML landmarks: `<main>`, `<nav>`, `<header>`, `<footer>`
- No `user-scalable=no` in viewport meta

**P2 — High priority:**
- Skip navigation link ("Skip to main content")
- Descriptive link text (not "click here")
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px
- `prefers-reduced-motion` media query
- ARIA labels on icon-only buttons (Instagram icon, hamburger menu)
- Google Maps iframe with `title` attribute

**P3 — Should have:**
- Content reflow without horizontal scroll at 320px
- Color is not the sole indicator of information
- Text spacing adjustable

**Target: Lighthouse Accessibility ≥ 95 on all pages.**

## Image Pipeline

### HEIC Handling

Tetiana's portfolio includes 8 HEIC files (iPhone format). Browsers don't support HEIC natively. Sharp's HEIC support on Windows is inconsistent.

**Recommended workflow:**
```
HEIC files (iPhone originals)
    ↓ npm script (heic-convert, quality 92)
JPG files (intermediate)
    ↓ placed in src/assets/
Astro <Image> component
    ↓ Sharp at build time
AVIF + WebP (automatically generated)
    ↓
dist/ → Cloudflare Pages
```

### Image Optimization

- Use Astro's `<Image>` component for all images — it handles format conversion, responsive srcset, and dimension attributes automatically
- Hero image: `fetchpriority="high"`, `loading="eager"`, preload via `<link rel="preload">`
- All other images: `loading="lazy"`
- Use `aspect-ratio` in CSS to reserve space and prevent CLS
- Serve AVIF as primary format (30-50% smaller than JPG), WebP as fallback

*Source: [Astro Image Optimization Guide](https://eastondev.com/blog/en/posts/dev/20251203-astro-image-optimization-guide/), [Astro Docs](https://docs.astro.build/en/guides/images/)*

# Site Architecture

## Verdict: Multi-Page (4 Pages)

### Why Multi-Page

| Factor | Single-Page | Multi-Page (4 pages) |
|---|---|---|
| SEO | One URL to rank | Multiple pages can rank for different queries |
| Content depth | Everything compressed | Each page serves a focused intent |
| Services section | Gets unwieldy in one scroll | Full detailed menu on its own page |
| Gallery | Limited within single page | Dedicated page with lightbox |
| Performance | Heavy single load | Smaller per-page payloads |
| Future growth | Hard to extend | Easy to add pages (blog, reviews) |

For a business with 10+ services, a portfolio gallery, booking instructions, and location information, **a single page would be too long and would sacrifice SEO** for important queries like "nail salon services National City."

### Recommended Page Set

| Page | URL | Primary Intent | Key Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Home** | `/` | Attract & orient | Hero, services teaser, gallery strip, about preview, CTA, map |
| **Services** | `/services` | Inform & convince | Full service menu by category, prices, durations, descriptions |
| **Gallery** | `/portfolio` | Prove quality | All portfolio images, lightbox, link to Instagram |
| **Book** | `/book` | Convert | Step-by-step booking instructions, CTA, map, address, FAQ |

### Content Hierarchy Per Page

**Home (`/`):**
1. Hero (Tetiana, headline, CTA)
2. Social proof strip
3. Services teaser (3-4 cards with "from" prices)
4. Gallery strip (6-9 images, horizontal scroll mobile)
5. About snippet (photo + 2 sentences)
6. Testimonials (2-3)
7. Instagram CTA band (full-width brand color)
8. Map + address
9. Footer

**Services (`/services`):**
1. Page header (h1)
2. Category: Manicure (h2) → service cards
3. Category: Pedicure (h2) → service cards
4. Category: Additional (h2) → service cards
5. Note about designs/add-ons
6. CTA: "Ready to book?"
7. Footer

**Gallery (`/portfolio`):**
1. Page header
2. Image grid (2-col mobile, 3-col tablet, 4-col desktop)
3. Lightbox on click (native `<dialog>`)
4. "See more on Instagram" link
5. CTA: "Love what you see?"

**Book (`/book`):**

2. How to Book (3 steps)

6. Location + map

# Anti-Patterns

## What NOT to Do

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| White text on brand pink (#FFC2D1) | WCAG fail, ADA lawsuit risk | Always use dark text (#1C1018) on brand color |
| Accessibility overlay widgets | 22.6% of sued sites had overlays; don't prevent lawsuits | Implement native WCAG compliance |
| Auto-rotating carousels | Users ignore them, hurt performance | Static hero with one strong image |
| Stock photography | Destroys trust, looks generic | Real photos of Tetiana, her work, her studio |
| Script/cursive fonts | Cheapens premium positioning | Cormorant Garamond (serif) for elegance |
| Hidden pricing | Creates friction, signals price insecurity | Full transparent pricing on /services |
| "Contact Us" as only CTA | Too vague, doesn't drive action | "Book via Instagram DM" — specific |
| No portfolio on homepage | Misses the #1 trust builder | Gallery strip visible within first 2 scrolls |
| No mobile optimization | Losing 70-85% of traffic | Mobile-first design approach |
| Embedded Instagram feed | Slow, API-dependent, shows competitor ads | Curated gallery + "See more on Instagram" link |
| Complex booking flow | Every additional step loses visitors | One-tap `ig.me/m/` deep link |
| Using `index.old` as basis | Outdated demo, doesn't reflect brand or strategy | Build from scratch per research + PRD |
| Generic "pamper yourself" copy | Every salon says it | Specific, confident brand voice from INFO.md |
| Google Analytics 4 for a static site | Overkill, requires consent banner | Cloudflare Web Analytics (free, cookie-free) |
| Multiple CTAs with different targets | Confuses visitor, dilutes conversion | One primary CTA (Instagram DM) everywhere |
| Popup on page load | Google penalizes intrusive interstitials | Inline CTAs within page flow |
| Ignoring `prefers-reduced-motion` | Accessibility violation + motion sickness | Always implement reduced motion media query |
| Too much pink | Looks juvenile, not premium | Pink as accent, cream/white as base |
